Recurring bills

The same bills, every month, already split.

Rent, Netflix, internet, electricity — set each one up once and it comes back every month on its own, already split your way. Scroll the household's list and you can see the rhythm: same bills, same shares, nobody re-entering anything.

  • Set any bill to repeat — monthly, weekly, whatever the cycle is
  • A recurring marker shows at a glance which bills are on autopilot
  • "Remind Me Before Due" nudges you ahead of the due date
  • Set an end date for a lease or a subscription you'll cancel
Uneven splits

Bigger room? Bigger share.

Rent is almost never a clean three-way split. Whoever took the master bedroom pays more, the box room pays less — set each person's percentage once and the numbers stop being a monthly argument.

  • Any ratio you agreed on — 50/20/30, or whatever your lease works out to
  • A live total confirms the moment the percentages reach 100%
  • Exclude a roommate from any single bill — the rest absorb it automatically
  • No sign-up for roommates — add anyone to a bill, even without the app
Household budgets

Know where the rent money goes.

See the whole household's month at a glance — what's gone to housing, utilities, food and everything else, each tracked against the budget you set together. No surprises when the bills land.

  • Spending broken down by category, month or year
  • A budget bar per category — see you're at 76% of housing before month-end
  • Charts reflect your share of the group's expenses, not just the raw total
Balances & settle-up

Nobody has to ask who owes what.

The running balance is right at the top of the group — who's up, who's down, and by how much. When it's time to square up, smart settle-up nets the whole household down to the fewest payments possible.

  • A running balance per roommate — tap any card to see the detail
  • Smart settle-up nets everyone down to the fewest payments possible
  • Owed money? The reminder bell nudges them instead of you having to
  • Real-time sync — everyone sees new bills the moment they're added
